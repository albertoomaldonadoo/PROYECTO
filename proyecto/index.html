HISTORY OF AIRCRAFT

Designed and built by Orville and Wilbur Wright, the Flyer I was the first heavier-than-air aircraft to fly successfully. The Wright brothers flew with it four times on December 17, 1903 at Kitty Hawk, in the state of North Carolina (USA).

The first airplane had a fir and ash wood structure and muslin cloth wings were some of the materials with which the first airplane was built. Thus, on October 17, 1903, after several failed attempts, the youngest of the brothers, Orville, boarded the Wright Flyer and made the first flight.

TYPES OF AIRCRAFT

Aircraft are classified into various categories based on their design, size, use and technical characteristics. The two main types of aircraft are fixed-wing and rotary-wing.

Fixed wing aircraft are those that maintain their aerodynamic shape thanks to a rigid structure and fixed wings. These aircraft are divided into various categories such as commercial aircraft, military aircraft, cargo aircraft and private aircraft. Commercial aircraft, such as the famous Boeing or Airbus, are designed to transport passengers on long-distance routes. Military aircraft, on the other hand, are used for defense and combat missions

ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES OF AIRPLANES

- Advantages:

1. It's easy and accessible: Although we sometimes complain that it can be crazy to buy a plane ticket, the truth is that it can seem that way for the simple fact of all the options available to us.

2. It is safe: Although plane accidents are dramatic, statistics confirm that it is much more likely to suffer an accident by car than by plane.

3. It's very fast: You may have the feeling that there is a long wait at the airport, but if you had to travel to New York by boat from Spain, it would surely seem silly to you to have to wait two hours before being able to get on the plane to change a trip of more than 10 days. by boat for a flight of about 7-8 hours.

- Disadvantages:

1. The location of airports: We have said that there are low-cost lines, but the truth is that these usually arrive and depart from secondary airports, so reaching the final destination sometimes involves having to take another means of transportation to conclude the trip.

2. Inconvenience: Pressurization may cause ear, headache or toothache in some passengers. Or also discomfort such as the feeling that your ears have been blocked, which is why it is sometimes difficult to use this means of transport to travel with small children, since it is difficult to explain to them what is happening.

3. Jet Lag: Due to the speed with which the plane allows us to travel, on long-distance trips we do not allow our body to get used to the time change little by little.
